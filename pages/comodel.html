Co-Model Application
A Companion Assistance for Concrete Model Designer
ROLE   UX/UI Designer, Independent Work       DATE   11/2016 – 12/2016       TOOL   Sketch, Proto.io, Illustrator
Problems Defined

To develop skills in reflective design, I participated a workshop that teaches making concrete models in the Architecture College. During the practice, I found some problems in the process of building a concrete model. One is that the process takes a long time span and is repeated, resulting to some students delay completing their models, or even abandon their unfinished models. Another one is that staff in the workshop have difficulty tracking which step each student’s work has finished in the long time span and how many students are ready to pour concrete a day, leading to problems in organizing classes and preparing enough materials.

Target Users
  • Students who want to help others create a model and students who need help finishing models.
  • Staff in Concrete Model Workshop who want to better organize classes and prepare enough materials
Insights and Solutions

Developed from my observation in the process, I came up with a mobile application called “Co-Model”.

  • It should serve as a medium by which students in the workshop can conveniently find others who are willing to help them do some steps of making a model.
  • It should be a platform of sharing working information in the process of making a concrete model so that students can easily find models that they want to contribute to, and staff can better organize the class and prepare materials for each student.
  • It should improve the process and support the concrete model community.

Main Walkthrough
  • Feature 1 - Updating List

    • Present all models that are updating working steps in the workshop. The process bar shows which steps the models are in. You can tap buttons “Ready” and “Requested” to get an experience of filtering results.

  • Feature 2 - Collaboration Details

    • Display concrete models made by a student, models the student contributed, and models the student liked. Clicking on a name of a model can open a detailed updating information of the model.

  • Feature 3 - Working Flow

    • Users first create a model project page, then they can edit this page by updating working steps and they can request for helps here. Other users can only view the page. But if the model is requesting for help, other users would be able to tap “contribute” and then they can update steps.
